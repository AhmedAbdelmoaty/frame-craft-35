# Analyst Triage — Development Roadmap from V1 to Full Product

## Roadmap Principle

Build gameplay logic first. Do not polish assets before the core analytical gameplay works.

Each version must answer one question.

## Version 0 — Documentation & Source of Truth

### Goal

Install this package and make Analyst Triage the source of truth.

### Includes

- master blueprint;
- prototype spec;
- feedback rules;
- roadmap;
- mission progression;
- Codex execution manual;
- task prompts;
- no-go rules;
- credit saver rules;
- small project skills.

### Excludes

- gameplay code;
- new assets;
- prototype implementation.

## Version 1 — Core Playable Moment

### Goal

Prove the game loop:

> company pressure → collect constraints → analyze two teams → recommend → consequence.

### Includes

- small company hub;
- Sales interaction;
- HR interaction;
- Analyst Office;
- Team A/B;
- Scan;
- Sort;
- Threshold;
- Outlier Mark;
- Impact Test;
- Reward/Training;
- win/fail feedback.

### Success test

A player understands through action that Team A’s higher mean is misleading and that Team B deserves reward.

## Version 1.5 — Feel & Feedback Pass

### Goal

Make V1 feel more like a game.

### Includes

- improved motion timing;
- better feedback for every action;
- more office life;
- stronger timer pressure;
- clearer win/fail consequences;
- improved card interactions.

### Excludes

- new missions;
- final assets;
- complex systems.

## Version 2 — Second Mission: Mean Is Sometimes Useful

### Goal

Prevent the wrong lesson “mean is always bad”.

### Mission

Three teams. One team has the highest mean and a stable distribution. The correct decision uses the mean because it represents the team.

### New concept

Mean is useful when distribution is stable and representative.

## Version 3 — Spread Mission

### Goal

Teach spread as risk/stability.

### Mission

Two or three teams with similar means but different spread. Correct recommendation depends on consistency and threshold alignment.

## Version 4 — Median / Skew Mission

### Goal

Teach center of distribution when data is skewed.

### Mission

Mean and median diverge. Player must notice body of team and median marker.

## Version 5 — Recommendation Complexity

### Goal

Move beyond reward/training only.

### New cards

- Reward
- Training
- Review
- Investigate
- Hold Decision

### Learning

Different patterns require different interventions.

## Version 6 — Data Quality Pressure

### Goal

Teach missing/partial data and sample risk.

### Mission

Some data is incomplete. Player must decide whether to delay, investigate, or make a limited recommendation.

## Version 7 — Vertical Slice

### Goal

Create a polished 20-30 minute slice.

### Includes

- 3-4 missions;
- polished company hub;
- stronger character presence;
- accepted temporary or semi-final assets;
- audio pass;
- learning recap after missions;
- stable build.

## Full Product Direction

Future product could include:

- more descriptive statistics concepts;
- multiple departments;
- campaign analysis;
- product category performance;
- branch comparisons;
- recurring stakeholders;
- mission scoring based on trust/fairness/impact;
- replayable data variants;
- professional facilitator mode for training.

## What Must Never Happen

- Do not add many missions before V1 is fun.
- Do not polish art before core action-feedback loop works.
- Do not add formulas before players need the concepts.
- Do not turn the game into BI dashboard training.
- Do not turn the player into a generic manager.
